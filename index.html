Mussolini and Fascism Study Sheet

Benito Mussolini and the Rise of Fascism

  • Benito Mussolini was an Italian politician who founded the fascist movement in Italy in 1919.
  • Fascism is a political ideology characterized by extreme nationalism, authoritarianism, and opposition to liberal democracy.
  • Mussolini became Italy's prime minister in 1922 after the "March on Rome," a show of force by his fascist supporters.
  • In 1925, Mussolini declared himself dictator and established a one-party state in Italy.

Mussolini's Policies and Actions

  • Mussolini's economic policies focused on state control of industry and a program of public works to boost employment.
  • Mussolini also pursued expansionist policies, invading Ethiopia in 1935 and Albania in 1939.
  • Mussolini allied Italy with Nazi Germany during World War II, but was eventually overthrown and executed by Italian partisans in 1945.

Fascist Ideology and Leaders

  • Fascist ideology emphasizes the importance of the nation-state, militarism, and the suppression of individual freedoms.
  • Fascism often uses propaganda, censorship, and violence to maintain power and suppress opposition.

Additional Events Regarding Mussolini

  • In 1912, Mussolini became the editor of a socialist newspaper in Italy, but he was expelled from the Socialist Party in 1914 for his support of Italy's involvement in World War I.
  • In 1919, Mussolini founded the Fasci Italiani di Combattimento, a political movement that would later become the National Fascist Party.
  • In 1929, Mussolini signed the Lateran Treaty with the Catholic Church, which recognized Vatican City as an independent state and granted the church financial compensation for the loss of its former territories.
  • In 1938, Mussolini enacted a series of anti-Semitic laws that stripped Italian Jews of their citizenship and prohibited intermarriage between Jews and non-Jews.
  • In 1940, Mussolini joined the Axis powers with Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan, leading Italy into World War II.
  • In 1943, Mussolini was overthrown and arrested by his own government, but was later rescued by German commandos and installed as the leader of the Italian Social Republic, a puppet state in northern Italy.
